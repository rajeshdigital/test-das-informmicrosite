1. Overview

Apprentices must be aged 16 or over and combine working with learning - from GCSEs or equivalent up to degree level.

Apprentices can be new or current employees.

If you hire an apprentice you must:

  • usually employ them for at least 30 hours per week
  • pay them at least the minimum wage for apprentices
  • offer them the same conditions and benefits offered to other employees working at a similar level
  • provide experience and opportunities to help with future progression
  • allow them to attend training and assessment during their working hours

Funding your apprenctices

Funding is available if you employ an apprentice in England.

If your organisation has a payroll of over £3 million, from April 2017 you will pay the apprenticeship levy to fund apprentices.

If you don’t pay the levy, you could get a grant or funding to employ an apprentice.

Hire an apprentice

How you employ an apprentice depends on whether your organisation pays the apprenticeship levy or not.

Levy payers must use the Digital Apprenticeship Service.

  1. 1 Choose training for your apprentice, and find a training provider, using the find apprenticeship training service.
  2. 2 Check if you’re eligible to get a grant.
  3. 3 Contact a training provider, they’ll handle your apprentice’s training, qualification and assessment.
  4. 4Advertise your apprenticeship - your training provider will do this for you through the find an apprenticeship service.
  5. 5 Select your apprentice and make an agreement with them.

To find out more about employing apprentices or providing traineeships, use the National Apprenticeship Service’s enquiry form. You can also call them using the number on the form.

How long it lasts

Apprenticeships can last from 1 to 6 years, depending on the level of qualification the apprentice is studying for.
